I Quit

September 27th, 2011 // Christopher Kennedy

That's right, I quit. I know I have said it before and talked about it many times, but I am really doing it this time. I am quitting freelancing.

Let me get right into it. I started working at M. Shanken Communications Inc. back in March as a lead UX strategist / front-end developer / designer / tall guy of the office. I went into my interview thinking that this is just another job. I had one interview with Mike Turro, the Director of Technology, and I knew that he would make one hell of a boss. He is one of the few people in this field that actual get it. I had that one interview and I was at my new desk shortly after.

After a few weeks of working here and getting to know the rest of the team, I fell in love. I have never had this feeling about a job that I wasn't the boss. My first real task was building an iPad app with the very talented Richard Ownes and working directly with the other member of the Wine Spectator family. This project really helped me fall in love with the rest of the company.

To those that know me and asked how I liked my new job were all in shock when I confessed my love. Honestly I have never really liked working for the man. But this man company treats me right.

The vibe of the company is a startup with old money, for the most part. Most of the people I work with come from startups which helps fuel this fire. This feeds my need for the startup world. I am very content with my position right now. I think it fits all of my needs that I long for.

Don't get me wrong, I will still attend hackathons and conferences, but I am going to limit my attendances to hackathons to a minimal, exclusively hacking with David Kay. I will always work on side projects, but I think they will be more influenced by on what is at hand and needed for the M. Shanken Dev Team.

I also plan on making more time for my lovely wife, that has been so patient with me over the years. She deserves at least an attentional 15 minutes a day. OK, maybe a bit more.

What about current projects or clients lined up? I will be terminating them over the next few days. I will point them in the right direction and wish them the best. I know this is a bit crazy, but I feel that this is only right. I fear my new direction is not fair to my clients if I can only give them partial commitments.

This is my birthday present to myself, which is today. I feel that I deserve to give myself something that will make me happy in the long run.

Thanks everyone for being so supportive.
